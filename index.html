I am a postdoc researcher at Centro de Modelamiento Matemático (CNRS IRL2807), Universidad de Chile (Santiago, Chili), supervised by Sebastian Donoso.

Previously I was a postdoc researcher at the Department of Mathematics in Université de Liège (Liège, Belgium), supervised by Julien Leroy.

In September 2022, I received my Ph.D. at LAMFA from Université de Picardie Jules Verne (Amiens, France) under the supervision of Samuel Petite.

In May 2019, I completed the Master 2 of Fundamental Mathematics at Sorbonne Université and in March 2018 I completed a Master's degree at Department of Mathematical Engineering (Universidad de Chile) under the direction of Alejandro Maass and Sebastian Donoso.

From January 2023 to June 2024, I organized the Discrete Mathematics's seminar at Université de Liège.

My research interests are mainly centered in Multidimensional symbolic dynamics (specifically minimal Cantor systems), Topological dynamics and Ergodic theory for Z^{d}-actions and its interactions with different fields of mathematics, such as automata theory, combinatorics on words, finite geometry, fractal geometry, and number theory.
